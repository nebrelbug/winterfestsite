Class graph (learn continually)

Before Christmas break, students were asked to personally rate their knowledge of programming on a scale of 1-10. approximately half the class reported a score of 2 or below. Students were also told, to give 20 hours of their winter break learning CSS, Javascript, and HTML using a free website called “Code academy.” Near the end of Winterim, students were again asked to rate their knowledge of programming, exactly like the beginning. The chart on the *left* shows what improvement students made during Winterim.

Personal websites (communicate effectively)

Using HTML, CSS, and Javascript, students spent hours coding from scratch, to create unique websites intended to better themselves and their community. On the very first day of Winterim, each student was assigned a partner, through time, each partnership had to learn how to communicate effectively, so that both individuals could benefit from the other. Most Compro students would agree, that most of the time used for their website creation was spent fixing code, and solving problems. If a problem were to occur, the students were instructed to try their hardest with google, before turning to thier partner, or asking others for help. Through this method, students have learned how to solve problems, through the resources they already have, the internet is not just used for entertainment.

Downtime activities

At the Adobe field trip, Compro learned to “work hard. Play hard.” Big companies like Adobe, know that hard work gets tedious, despite the endless capabilities of programming. All students know that lunch breaks are crucial in any work environment. Winterim wasn't all work, the entire class have enjoyed multiple lunches, and snacks provided by Winterim fees, “Papa John’s” and “Panda express” were the favorites! Compro have watched many episodes of “Splatalot” (on BYU-TV) and played multiple indoor frisbee games with Mr.Gilbert.

Visitors

Prior to the course the students were asked to rate their programming abilities on scale from 1 -10 (1 = no knowledge, 10 = I tutor Bill Gates on the weekend). About half of the class rated themselves at a level 1 or 2. By the end of Winterim every student reported a higher score. Over time, students have recognized the importance of effective communication, and passion, while coding a website.
